Genetic Information (Protein & Others)

Protein Synthesis
-----------------

Genetic Code 
^^^^^^^^^^^^

- **Codon**

  - **General**: *triplet of nucleotides composed of AGCU in mRNA (from 5' to 3')*
  - **Translation**: *each codon determine an amino acid, UAA & UAG & UGA are termination codon*
  - **Characteristic**

    - **Specificity**: *a specific codon always code the same amino acid*
    - **Universality**: *genetic code is universal in all organism*
    - **Degeneracy**: *multiple codons code single amino acid*
    - **Commaless**: *code is read continuously without overlap*

- **Anticodon**

  - **General**: *triplet of nucleotide on tRNA*
  - **Binding with codon**: *complementary, antiparallel*
  - **Wobble hypothesis**: *3rd base of codon (1st base of anticodon) allow nontraditional pairing, single tRNA recognize multiple codon*

- **Mutation**

  - **Silent mutation**: *changed base code for the same amino acid*
  - **Missense mutation**: *changed base code for different amino acid*
  - **Nonsense mutation**: *changed base code for termination codon*
  - **Trinucleotide repeat expansion**: *triplet of base repeated im tandem (ex: Huntingtin disease)*
  - **Splice site mutation**: *intron removing altered*
  - **Frameshift mutation**: *reading frame altered*

Component of Translation 
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

- **Amino acid**: *all amino acid must be present*
- **Transfer RNA**

  - **Amino acid attachment site**: *attachment of specific amino acid at 3' end, through ester linkage (carboxyl group of amino acid, 3'hydroxyl group of nucleotide)*
  - **Anticodon**: *triplet of nucleotide, pair with specific codon*

- **Aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase**: *recognize & catylize convalent attachment between specific amino acid & tRNA, require ATP*
- **Messenger RNA**: *template for synthesis of polypeptide, prokaryotes: polycistronic, eukaryotes: monocistronic*
- **Ribosome**

  - **Subunit**: *prokaryotes: 70S = 30S + 50S, eukaryotes: 80S = 40S + 60S*
  - **Component**: *ribosomal RNA, ribosomal protein*
  - **A P E site**: *A site: incoming aminoacyl-tRNA, P site: peptidyl-tRNA, E site: empty tRNA*
  - **Location**: *associate with ER (synthesize protein to external & membrane & lysosome), free in cytosol (synthesize protein to cytosol & nucleus & mitochondria)*

- **Protein factor**: *required for initiation & elongation & termination*
- **Energy source**: *ATP & GTP*

Step in Translation
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

- **Initiation**

  - **Step 1**: *mRNA bind with small ribosomal subunit*
  
    - **Prokaryotes**: *Shine-Dalgarno sequence base-pair with 30S subunit*
    - **Eukaryotes**: *5'cap bind with 40S subunit, require ATP*
  
  - **Step 2** : *initiation codon AUG recognized by initiator tRNA, first amino acid is methionine (later removed)*

    - **Prokaryotes**: *fascilated by IF-2 & GTP, initiator tRNA carry formylated methionine (methylated by N10-formyl tetrahydrofolate)*
    - **Eukaryotes**: *fascilated by eIF-2 & GTP, initiator tRNA carry methionine*

  - **Step 3**: *large ribosomal subunit join, initiator tRNA at P site*

- **Elongation**

  - **Step 1**: *corresponding aminoacyl-tRNA bind to A site, fascilated by EF & GTP*
  - **Step 2**: *peptide bond form between carboxyl group of P site amino acid & amino group of A site amino acid, catalyzed by peptidyl transferase (in rRNA)*
  - **Step 3**: *ribosome translocate & advance 3 nucleotide (P site → E site, A site → P site), fascilated by EF & GTP*

- **Termination**

  - **Step 1**: *termination codon UAA & UAG & UGA recognized by RF*
  - **Step 2**: *peptide release from ribosome*

- **Protein folding**: *spontaneous or fascilated by chaperone*
- **Protein targeting**: *protein contain sequence directing it to final location*

  - **Secreted protein**: *N-terminal hydrophobic signal sequence recognized by SRP, deliver peptide into RER via SRP receptor & translocon*
  - **Nuclear protein**: *internal basic signal sequence*
  - **Mitochondrial protein**: *N-terminal amphipathic signal sequence*
  - **Peroxisomal protein**: *C-terminal tripeptide signal sequence*

(Co)Posttranslational Modification
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

- **Trimming**: *inactive precusor cleaved to release active protein, cleavage in RER or Golgi or vesicle or external*
- **Covalent attachment**

  - **Phosphorylation**: *on hydroxyl group of serine or threonine, increase or decrease protein activity*
  - **Glycosylation**: *on amide nitrogen of asparagine (N linked, in RER) or hydroxyl group of serine or threonine (O linked, in Golgi)*
  - **Hydroxylation**: *on proline & lysine residue of collagen, by vitamin C-dependent hydroxylase*
  - **Others**: *carboxyl group (blood-clotting protein), biotin (carboxylation enzyme), lipid (anchored protein), acetyl group*

- **Protein degradation**: *protein attached by ubiquitin, degraded by proteasome*

Regulation of Gene Expression 
-----------------------------

General
^^^^^^^

- **Gene type**

  - **Constituve**: *expressed at constant level*
  - **Regulated**: *expressed under certian condition*

- **Regulation site**

  - **Prokaryotes**: *transcription*
  - **Eukaryotes**: *transcription, post transcriptional & translational process, access to DNA*

- **Regulatory element**

  - **cis-acting**: *regulatory DNA sequence, bound by regulatory molecule*
  - **trans-acting**: *regulatory molecule, bind DNA through motifs such as zinc finger or leucine zipper or helix-turn-helix*

Prokaryotic Regulation
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

- **Operon**

  - **General**
  
    - **Definition**: *coordinatedly regulated group of gene*
    - **Operator**: *DNA segment regulating gene activity*
    - **Repressor**: *bind operator, block RNA polymerase*
    - **Inducer**: *bind to repressor, repressor cannot bind operator*

  - **Lactose operon**

    - **Gene**: *lacZ (hydrolyze lactose), lacY (movement of lactose), lacA (acetylate lactose)*
    - **Negative control**: *when lactose present, allolactose bind to repressor, facilitate transcription by inhibiting repressor binding operator*
    - **Positive control**: *when glucose absent, cAMP-CAP complex bind to CAP site, facilitate transcription*

  - **Tryptophan operon**

    - **Gene**: *5 gene coding for enzyme required in tryptophan synthesis*
    - **Negative control**: *when tryptophan present, tryptophan bind to repressor, inhibit transcription by facilate repressor binding operator*
    - **Attenuation**: *when tryptophan plentiful, hairpin in mRNA form, terminate transcription*

- **Others**

  - **Stringent response**: *amino acid starvation cause synthesis of ppGpp, ppGpp bind RNA pol & inhibit rRNA synthesis*
  - **Ribosomal protein**: *low rRNA cause r-protein bind its own mRNA (at Shine-Dalgarno sequence), inhibit transcription of r-protein mRNA*
